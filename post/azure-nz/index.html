Azure NZ Region

Microsoft clears final hurdle with Overseas Investment Office approval

Posted by TW on Tuesday, September 1, 2020

With the recent Overseas Investment Office approval, Microsoft is getting closer to deploying Azure datacentres in New Zealand. The NZ Azure Region is likely still a year or two away from going live, but is keenly anticipated.

The new Azure footprint here is rumoured to be two new physical datacentre builds both in Auckland.

A couple of early cornerstone business clients have committed to NZ Azure including Fonterra, not to mention whatever the government has promised. My sense is there is significant pent up demand that should see significant uptake in the NZ Azure region. This will include interested customers who already determined AU was just too far away, had data sovereignty issues, or already have workloads running in AU or other regions and will “bring then home” as soon as the they can. Unknown at this stage whether Microsft365 services will also be run from NZ region, but if so I would expect lots of NZ customers would also want their services re-homed locally.

NZ had the highest uptake per capita in the world for Office365 services, and Microsoft would probably be the strongest public cloud provider in NZ.

Microsofts capacity planning will need to be on form, I anticipate huge demand right from launch.
